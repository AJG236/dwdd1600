Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Primary author of the Declaration. Later became the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
A leading advocate for independence in the Continental Congress. Later became the 2nd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Renowned diplomat, inventor, and statesman. Helped negotiate the alliance with France and the Treaty of Paris.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress at the time of signing. Famous for his large, bold signature, symbolizing defiance to the British crown.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
A key organizer of colonial resistance, including the Boston Tea Party. Cousin to John Adams and influential in rallying support for independence.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
Only person to sign all four great state papers of the U.S. Played a key role in shaping the new government.
Robert Morris (Pennsylvania)
Financier of the Revolution; helped fund the Continental Army. Later helped organize the nation's finances under the new government.

These figures were pivotal not only in declaring independence but also in shaping the early United States.
